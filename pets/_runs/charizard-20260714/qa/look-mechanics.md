# Charizard look mechanics

Charizard keeps both feet and lower torso anchored on a shared baseline. The neck, head, eyes, horns, wings, and flame tip carry attention: the eyes lead; the head and snout turn or pitch subtly; wings follow with small balanced shifts; the tail stays attached with a slight delayed curve. No whole-sprite rotation.

- `000` up: snout and eyes raised, eyelids open upward, upper chest subtly lifted.
- `090` screen-right: snout, pupils, horns, and nearer wing angle unmistakably toward screen-right.
- `180` down: snout dips, eyes look down, shoulders compress slightly while feet stay planted.
- `270` screen-left: mirror of the right-facing family, with snout, pupils, horns, and nearer wing toward screen-left.

Each 22.5-degree step shifts the head and gaze evenly between adjacent cardinal families. Preserve orange scales, cream belly, teal wing membranes, tail flame, proportions, and baseline.
